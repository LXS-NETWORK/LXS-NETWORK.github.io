How it works

Everything about LXS, in plain language — what it is, how coins are created, how mining and the pool work, how tokens live on the chain, and how to verify all of it yourself. Prefer quick answers? Jump to the FAQ.

What is LXS?

LXS is a proof-of-work Layer-1 blockchain — its own independent network, like Bitcoin, not a token on someone else's chain. Coins are created only by mining, so there's no hidden faucet of insider coins. Anyone can run the software, mine the coin, launch a token, and send value, without asking permission.

If you know Bitcoin, LXS will feel familiar: the same idea of a coin earned with real work and secured by miners, with no company in the middle. What LXS adds is a one-click token launchpad, so any community can create and trade its own token on top of the chain. The website you're reading is just a convenient window — the real network is the blocks and the nodes that hold them, and it keeps running with or without this page.

How mining works

Mining is the process of spending computing power to confirm transactions, secure the network, and keep everyone in agreement — without any central authority. Miners race to solve a hard puzzle; the first to solve it adds the next block and collects its reward. Rewriting history would mean out-working the entire network at once, which is what makes the chain secure.

You mine by running the miner app (see the Mine page) — on an ordinary CPU, no special hardware. Nodes find each other through a distributed peer directory (DHT), so there's no central server to shut down: the network keeps going as long as anyone, anywhere, is running a node. New here? You can start mining without understanding any of the technical details.

The mining pool

Finding a block solo is like a lottery — rare, but the whole 50 LXS is yours. A pool is a group of miners who combine their computing power and split the rewards in proportion to the work each contributes, so everyone gets steady payouts instead of waiting for a rare solo win.

When you pool-mine, your machine submits shares — proof that it's doing real work toward finding a block. When the pool wins a block, the reward is split in proportion to the shares each miner contributed (a scheme called PPLNS), and paid to your wallet automatically. The LXS pool charges 0% fee, and payouts wait about 50 minutes (12 blocks) to mature so a chain reorganisation can't pay out a reward the pool never received. Connect with lxs mine -pool <url> or just pick “pool” in the miner app.

Tokenomics

  • 50 LXS per block goes to the miner who wins it (in a pool, split by contributed work).
  • The reward halves every 1,000,000 blocks — roughly one era every ~7.6 years — so issuance slows predictably over time. No one can change this.
  • About 100 million LXS is mined out over roughly 500 years, plus a 20 million genesis allocation to the founder, for a maximum of about 120 million LXS.
  • 20% of every transaction fee is burned (destroyed forever); the rest goes to miners.

New LXS is created at a decreasing, predictable rate that no one can alter. Fees reward the people securing the chain; the burn slowly removes coins from supply. None of this promises a price — it just describes exactly how coins enter and leave circulation.

The founder allocation

20,000,000 LXS were created at genesis for the founder. There was no presale, no ICO, no VC round, and no whitelist — everything beyond that founder allocation is mined by whoever shows up.

That allocation is fixed and written into the genesis block — the same lxs-genesis.json that ships inside every miner download — so anyone can verify the exact amount. As ~100M more LXS is mined over the coming centuries, the founder's share becomes a smaller and smaller fraction of the total.

How tokens work

A token you launch is an on-chain smart contract. Once created, it lives on the blockchain and is replicated across every node — it is not stored on any website or company server. The Tokens page just reads it from the chain and draws it for you.

Every new token is instantly tradeable on a bonding curve: the contract itself sets the price and lets anyone buy or sell with LXS, so there's no need for upfront liquidity or a listing. Because the token lives on-chain, it stays alive even if this website disappears.

The free-gas faucet

Doing anything on-chain (like creating your first token) costs a tiny amount of LXS for gas. A brand-new wallet has zero, so new users can click “Get LXS for gas (free)” on the Tokens page to claim a small amount — just enough to afford that first action. One claim per address.

Verify it yourself

Everything about LXS is open and checkable:

  • Read the code. The full node, miner, launchpad, and pool are open-source at github.com/LXS-NETWORK/LXS.
  • Run a node. Download the same binary everyone else runs, join the network, and see the chain for yourself.
  • Check the balances. Add LXS to MetaMask and look up any address on the chain.
  • Mine a block. The surest proof the network is real and open is to earn a coin from it yourself.

Keep your coins safe

LXS is non-custodial: your key lives on your device, not on a server. That means you're in control — and also responsible. Whoever holds your recovery phrase or private key owns your coins, and no one can recover them for you.

  • Back up your recovery phrase offline and never share it. No real LXS tool will ever ask for it.
  • Ignore anyone promising guaranteed returns or to “double” your LXS.
  • Double-check addresses before sending; on-chain transfers can't be reversed.
