How it works

Everything about LXS, in plain language — what it is, how the coins are created, how to connect it, how mining works, and how tokens live on the chain.

What is LXS?

LXS is a proof-of-work layer-1 blockchain — its own independent network, like Bitcoin, not a token on someone else's chain. Coins are created only by mining, so there's no hidden faucet of insider coins. Anyone can run the software, mine the coin, launch a token, and send value, without asking permission.

The website you're reading is just a convenient window. The real network is the thousands of blocks and the nodes that hold them — it keeps running with or without this page.

Tokenomics

  • 50 LXS per block is paid to the miner who wins it.
  • The reward halves every 1,000,000 blocks — roughly one era every ~7.6 years — so issuance slows down over time.
  • About 100 million LXS is mined out over roughly 500 years, plus a 20 million genesis allocation, for a maximum of about 120 million LXS.
  • 20% of transaction fees are burned (destroyed), and the rest go to miners.

Fees reward the people doing the work of securing the chain; the burn slowly removes coins from supply. No part of this promises a price — it just describes how coins enter and leave circulation.

How mining works

LXS uses proof-of-work: miners race to solve a hard math puzzle, and the first to solve it gets to add the next block and collect its reward. This is what secures the chain — rewriting history would mean out-working the entire honest network.

You mine by running a node (see the Mine page). Nodes find each other through a DHT (a distributed peer directory), so the network is decentralized: there's no central server to shut down, and it keeps going as long as anyone, anywhere, is running a node.

How tokens work

A token you launch is an on-chain smart contract. Once created, it lives on the blockchain and is replicated across every node — it is not stored on any website or company server. The Tokens page just reads it from the chain and draws it for you.

Every new token is instantly tradeable on a bonding curve: the contract itself sets the price and lets anyone buy or sell with LXS, so there's no need for upfront liquidity or a listing. Because the token lives on-chain, it stays alive even if this website disappears.

The free-gas faucet

Doing anything on-chain (like creating your first token) costs a tiny amount of LXS for gas. A brand-new wallet has zero. So new users can click “Get LXS for gas (free)” on the Tokens page to claim a small amount — just enough to afford that first action. One claim per address.
