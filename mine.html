Mine LXS

Point your computer at the network and earn LXS for the blocks you win. There's an easy way and a technical way — pick whichever fits you.

Download the miner

Grab the app for your operating system:

Unzip and run the launcher inside (START-MINING). It asks for your address — paste one, or press Enter to create a wallet. Windows/macOS may warn about an unsigned app; the folder's READ-ME-FIRST explains the one click to allow it.

The easy way

For most people, mining is four steps:

  1. Download the miner above and open it.
  2. Pick pool or solo — the app asks first. Not sure? Pick pool.
  3. Paste your LXS address — this is where your rewards go. Don't have one? The app can create a wallet for you and show you the address to save.
  4. Watch your balance — it grows as you mine.

Pool or solo?

Pool mining (recommended) — everyone's computers work as one team. When the pool wins a block, the 50 LXS is split among the miners in proportion to the work each one contributed, and paid to your wallet automatically. A normal laptop earns a little on every pool block instead of waiting to win one alone — steady income, small slices.

Solo mining — your machine competes by itself. You keep the whole 50 LXS each time you win a block — but only then. Rewards are lumpy: some days a few, some days none. That's normal proof-of-work, and the right choice for powerful hardware.

Honest note: pool payouts wait ~50 minutes (12 blocks) before paying, so a chain reorganisation can't pay out coins the pool never got. The pool takes 0% fee — the whole reward goes to the miners.

The technical way

Advanced users can run the node directly from the binary or source. Get it from the repo: github.com/LXS-NETWORK/LXS.

Pool mining (no sync, no datadir needed — just hash and get paid):

lxs mine -pool https://lxsnetwork.duckdns.org -coinbase <YOUR_ADDRESS>

Solo mining (runs a full node):

lxs mine -coinbase <YOUR_ADDRESS> -genesis lxs-genesis.json -p2p-port 30303 \
  -bootstrap /ip4/79.72.25.166/tcp/30303/p2p/12D3KooWRSSSocSqWG978SWKpimQsti4WkmjytQX7g1qgJKnNzuA

The lxs-genesis.json file ships inside every download above. The built-in binary already knows the seed, so -bootstrap is optional — the flag is shown for clarity.

What each flag does:

  • -coinbase <YOUR_ADDRESS> — the address that receives your block rewards.
  • -bootstrap <SEED> — a seed peer to join the network through (how your node finds others).
  • -genesis genesis.json — the genesis file that defines the chain you're joining.
  • -p2p-port 30303 — the port your node listens on for peer connections.

Once running, your node syncs with peers and starts mining to your coinbase address — same rewards, just from the command line.
